I analyzed the data and accounted for the mid-month meter swaps.

## Findings

- **Readings**: 30 washers, 3-hour pulse-register readings, all marked `OK`. The scheduled window runs from the first reading (`2026-06-01 00:00:00`) to the last (`2026-06-30 21:00:00`).
- **Meter swaps**: The service log shows 10 washers had a scheduled pulse-register exchange on 2026-06-15 12:00. On those machines the cumulative register **restarts on the new meter** (e.g. WASH-007's old meter reads 1156, then the new meter begins at 6 at the same instant).

## Method

Naively taking `last − first` per machine treats the register restart as a huge drop and **undercounts** (gives only 48,123). The correct approach sums each meter serial's own `last − first` and adds the two segments for swapped machines:

- Non-swapped machine: `last − first`
- Swapped machine: `(old-meter last − old-meter first) + (new-meter last − new-meter first)`

All registers are monotonic within each serial, so the arithmetic is clean.

**Total = 60,801 gallons** across all 30 washers for the June reporting window.
