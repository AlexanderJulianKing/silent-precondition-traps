**What's going on:** the README says `client_seq` is the *device event counter* — i.e. the client's own identity for each event it emits. So a single client event is identified by `(user_id, client_seq)`, not by `event_id` (which is just the per-ingestion row ID).

- Every duplicate pair has the **same screen** but a **different `event_id`**, and the second copy lands ~90 seconds after the first.

That's the textbook signature of at-least-once delivery — the device event was transmitted, retried, and ingested twice under a fresh `event_id`. Counting distinct client events removes the 404 redundant deliveries:

`40,404 raw rows − 404 duplicate re-ingestions = 40,000 actual client events`
