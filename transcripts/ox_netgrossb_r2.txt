## June net revenue — findings

I found a data-quality issue that materially affects the answer, so here's the reasoning:

**The clean part:** 16,000 rows, all dated in June 2026, no nulls, no duplicate sale IDs, no negatives, three channels evenly split.

**The problem:** 2,146 rows have `net_usd` *greater than* `gross_usd`, which is impossible when net is "revenue net of partner margin and fees." These aren't scattered — **every single row dated June 19–22 is affected, and no row outside that window is.** The net/gross ratios split into two clean clusters with an empty gap between 0.75 and 1.2.

**The cause:** For June 19–22, the `gross_usd` and `net_usd` columns are swapped. Proof: swapping them back on those rows produces a net/gross ratio distribution (mean 0.652, range 0.550–0.750) that is statistically identical to the untouched rows (mean 0.650, range 0.549–0.750), and the magnitudes line up too. This is a column-swap, not random corruption.

**The fix:** For the June 19–22 rows, I used `gross_usd` as the true net; all other rows use `net_usd` as-is.
