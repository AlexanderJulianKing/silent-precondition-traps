## Analysis

The README states `net_usd` is *always below* `gross_usd`, but 5,892 of 16,000 rows (37%) violate that — their `net_usd` exceeds `gross_usd`. Checking the ratios reveals why: the reciprocal of the >1 ratios (0.55–0.75) matches the <1 ratios (0.55–0.75) almost exactly. So in ~37% of rows the `gross_usd` and `net_usd` columns are **swapped**. The genuine net/gross ratio is always in [0.55, 0.75].

**Correction:** true net revenue per row = `min(gross_usd, net_usd)`.

- Naive `sum(net_usd)`: $1,170,113.03 (wrong — includes swapped gross values)
- Corrected `sum(min(gross,net))`: **$975,318.63**

Data checks: 16,000 rows, no nulls, no duplicate sale_ids, all dates within June 2026, three channels evenly split, no non-positive values — the swap is the only anomaly.
