## Analysis

I loaded `disputes_june.csv` with pandas (5,200 rows, one per dispute, all dated within June 2026, no duplicate `dispute_id`s). Data quality on `merchant_code` was clean — no stray whitespace, consistent 4-letter uppercase codes — with one important exception.

**Merchant-code dispute counts (June):**

The largest bucket (683) is the literal token `NULL` — a placeholder for disputes where no merchant code was captured, not an actual merchant. Per the README, `merchant_code` is the short code assigned at onboarding, so `NULL` rows are unassigned/missing and don't represent a merchant. (They're worth flagging separately for risk review — 13% of June disputes lack a merchant code.)

Excluding that sentinel, the merchant with the most disputes is **RUBY** with **494**.
